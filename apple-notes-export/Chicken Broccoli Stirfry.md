Chicken Broccoli Stirfry

Ingredients

- [ ] 150 g dry rice noodles (medium-width)
- [x] 400 g chicken breast, thinly sliced
- [ ] 1 broccoli separated into bite-sized florets
- [ ] 1 medium carrot (about 100 g), julienned
- [ ] 2 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- [ ] 2 tbsp neutral oil, divided
- [ ] 1 tsp sesame oil (to finish)
- [ ] 1 tsp sesame seeds (garnish)
- [ ] Optional heat: 1–2 tsp sriracha or 2 tsp Lao Gan Ma chili crisp (to taste)

Sauce

- [x] 2 tbsp soy sauce
- [x] 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- [x] 1 tsp sugar
- [x] 100 ml water or low-sodium chicken stock
- [x] 2 tsp cornstarch

Quick marinade for chicken

- [x] 1 tsp soy sauce
- [x] 1 tsp rice vinegar
- [x] 1 tsp cornstarch
- [x] 1 tsp neutral oil

Instructions

- [x] Soak rice noodles in very hot (not boiling) water for 6–8 minutes until
      just pliable but still a touch firm. Drain and rinse with cold water. Toss
      with 1 tsp oil to prevent sticking.
- [x] In a bowl, mix sliced chicken with soy sauce, vinegar, cornstarch, and
      oil. Set aside 10 minutes while you prep veg.
- [x] In a cup, whisk soy sauce, rice vinegar, oyster/hoisin (if using), sugar,
      pepper, water/stock, and cornstarch until smooth.
- [x] Heat a wok or large skillet over medium-high. Add 1 tbsp neutral oil.
- [x] Add chicken in a single layer. Sear 2–3 minutes, flip, and cook just until
      no longer pink. Remove to a plate.
- [x] Add remaining 1 tbsp oil. Add ginger and garlic; stir 10–15 seconds until
      fragrant.
- [x] Add broccoli and carrot. Stir-fry 2–3 minutes. Splash in 2 tbsp water,
      cover (or just toss) 1 minute to steam-tender.
- [x] Return chicken to the pan. Give the sauce a quick stir (cornstarch
      settles), pour it in, and bring to a simmer. It should thicken glossy in
      30–60 seconds.
- [x] Add the drained noodles. Toss gently 1–2 minutes until the noodles are hot
      and evenly coated. If too thick, add 1–2 tbsp water; if too loose, simmer
      30 seconds more.
- [ ] Off heat, stir in sesame oil. Add sriracha or chili crisp to taste.
