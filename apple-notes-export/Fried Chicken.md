Fried Chicken

Ingredients

- [ ] 1 whole chicken, cut up (or ~8 pieces of your favorite cuts of chicken)
- [ ] 2 teaspoons kosher salt
- [ ] 2 teaspoons garlic powder
- [ ] 2 teaspoons onion powder
- [ ] 480 mL buttermilk (2 cups)
- [ ] 2 Tablespoons hot sauce
- [ ] 250 g all-purpose flour (2 cups)
- [ ] 120 g cornstarch (1 cup)
- [ ] 2 teaspoons kosher salt
- [ ] 2 teaspoons paprika
- [ ] 2 teaspoons black pepper
- [ ] peanut oil (for frying)
- [ ] flaked salt (for serving, optional)

Instructions

- [ ] Place 1 whole chicken, cut up, in a large bowl. Sprinkle on top of the
      chicken: 2 teaspoons kosher salt, 2 teaspoons garlic powder, and 2
      teaspoons onion powder, then toss to coat.
- [ ] Add 480 mL buttermilk and 2 Tablespoons hot sauce; stir until combined.
      Cover and marinate in the fridge at least 2 hours (overnight is best).
- [ ] In a medium bowl, whisk together 250 g all-purpose flour, 120 g
      cornstarch, 2 teaspoons kosher salt, 2 teaspoons paprika, and 2 teaspoons
      black pepper. Set aside.
- [ ] Fill a deep fryer with peanut oil (or another neutral frying oil) and
      preheat to 163°C. You may need to turn down to 149°C if the skin is
      darkening too quickly.
- [ ] Also, if using a cast iron skillet, monitor closely since it holds heat
      well.
- [ ] Take chicken pieces one at a time, let excess buttermilk drip off, then
      coat in the flour mixture. Shake off excess. Let sit a few minutes until
      the coating looks a little pasty.
- [ ] Carefully add chicken to the fryer. Don’t add more than 3–4 pieces at a
      time; fry in batches. Fry until golden brown, turning every few minutes.
- [ ] Chicken is done when the internal temperature reaches 74°C. Estimates:
      dark meat ~12–14 min; white meat ~8–10 min (use a thermometer).
- [ ] Drain on a paper towel–lined tray and immediately sprinkle with a little
      flaked salt. Repeat with remaining pieces.
