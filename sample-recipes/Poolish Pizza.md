# Poolish Pizza  
  
## Ingredients  
### Poolish  
- [x] Water - 250g  
- [x] Yeast - 0.4g  
- [x] Flour - 250g  
  
### Final Dough  
- [x] Water - 175g (very cold)  
- [x] Salt - 14g  
- [x] Yeast - 0.25g  
- [x] Flour - 400g  
  
  
## Instructions  
### For the Poolish  
- [x] Mix the water, flour and yeast together to form a smooth paste. Cover the container and let ferment at 21°C for 7-8 hours. The Poolish is ready when it has tripled in size, and it’s bubbly, but hasn't collapsed at all.  
  
### For the Final Dough  
- [x] Add the second measurement of water to a large mixing bowl.  
- [x] Add the Poolish, second measurement of yeast and salt, then stir the mixture with a fork to combine evenly.  
- [x] Add the second measurement of flour and mix with the fork until you have a shaggy pizza dough. Next, use a wet hand and continue mixing the dough until it is relatively homogenous. Cover and let the dough rest for 10-15 minutes.   
- [x] Stretch and fold 1  
- [x] Stretch and fold 2  
- [x] Coil fold  
- [x] Form the dough into a ball, place it into a lightly oiled container, cover it, and let the dough proof at 21°C for 2 hours.   
- [x] Next, divide the dough into 4 x 275 gram pieces. Ball them up and place them into lightly oiled container. Rest for 30 minutes, then place the dough in the refrigerator overnight to cold ferment. The dough is ready to bake for dinner the following evening. Remember to pull the dough 1-1.5 hours before stretching.
