Japanese milk bread (Shokupan)

Ingredients

- [ ] 20g flour (for tangzhong)
- [ ] 50g water (for tangzhong)
- [ ] 50g milk (for tangzhong)
- [ ] 120g milk
- [ ] 4g yeast
- [ ] 330 g flour
- [ ] 20g sugar
- [ ] 7g salt
- [ ] 1 egg
- [ ] 20g butter, softened

Instructions

- [ ] Add the water and milk to a pot and whisk in 20g flour. Set the pot over
      medium-low heat and stir the mixture until the roux thickens to a thick,
      pudding-like consistency. Set aside and let cool to 40C.
- [ ] Heat the milk for 45 seconds in the microwave (roughly around 40 degrees
      F). Stir in the yeast and set aside.
- [ ] Meanwhile, add the 330g of flour, sugar, and salt to a large mixing bowl.
      Add the milk mixture, the cooled tangzhong, and crack in 1 egg. Mix in
      stand mixer with hook attachment for around 5 minutes, then let rest for
      15 minutes to allow the dough to relax. Once rested, continue kneading in
      the bowl for another 10 or so minutes or until it passes the window pane
      test.
- [ ] Once done kneading, add the dough to the mixing bowl and cover with
      plastic wrap. Let rise until roughly doubled in size, about 60-90 minutes.
      It could take more or less time depending on the yeast, room/dough
      temperature.
- [ ] Once doubled in size, punch the dough down and turn out onto the counter.
      Work the dough into a large rectangle about the width of the pan and then
      tightly roll the loaf up. Pinch the seam at the bottom and place seam side
      down into a nonstick or greased loaf pan. Cover lightly with plastic wrap
      and let rise for another 45 to 60 minutes until the dough has risen above
      the top edge of the loaf pan.
- [ ] Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit with 15-20 minutes left of
      proofing.
- [ ] Whisk an egg yolk with a splash of milk. Brush over the top of the proofed
      loaf.
- [ ] Add the pan to the oven and set a timer for 30 minutes. The exterior of
      the bread should be golden brown. Slide the loaf onto a wire rack and let
      cool before slicing and enjoying.
