Morrocan-style Stew

Ingredients

- [ ] 1.5–2 lb (680–900 g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1–1.25
      inch pieces
- [ ] 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
- [ ] 1 large onion, diced
- [ ] 2 medium carrots, diced
- [ ] 3 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 2 tbsp tomato paste
- [ ] 1 can crushed or diced tomatoes
- [ ] 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- [ ] 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- [ ] 1 red bell pepper, diced (optional)
- [ ] 1 small zucchini, diced (optional)
- [ ] 1.5 tsp ground cumin
- [ ] 1 tsp smoked paprika
- [ ] 1 tsp ground coriander
- [ ] 1/4–1/2 tsp cinnamon
- [ ] 1 bay leaf
- [ ] 1/2–1 tsp chili flakes (optional)
- [ ] Salt and black pepper
- [ ] 1/2 lemon (juice), chopped parsley or cilantro to finish

Instructions

- [ ] Brown chicken:
- [ ] Pat thighs dry; season with 1 tsp kosher salt and pepper.
- [ ] Heat 1 tbsp oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high. Sear chicken in 2
      batches until lightly browned (5–7 min total). Remove.
- [ ] Build base:
- [ ] Add 1 tbsp oil. Sauté onion and carrots with a pinch of salt 4–5 min.
- [ ] Stir in garlic, cumin, paprika, coriander, cinnamon, and chili flakes;
      cook 30–60 sec until fragrant.
- [ ] Add tomato paste; cook 1 min.
- [ ] Simmer:
- [ ] Add tomatoes, broth, chickpeas, bay leaf, and chicken (plus any juices).
- [ ] Bring to a boil, then simmer gently 20–25 min until chicken is tender and
      stew thickens.
- [ ] Add bell pepper/zucchini in the last 10–12 min.
- [ ] Finish and serve:
- [ ] Season to taste with salt and pepper.
- [ ] Stir in lemon juice and parsley/cilantro.
- [ ] Serve with couscous, rice, or crusty bread; dollop of plain yogurt
      optional.
