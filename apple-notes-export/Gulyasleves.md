Gulyasleves

Ingredients

For the soup

- [ ] 600g beef, in small cubes
- [ ] 3 medium onions (about 300 g total), finely chopped
- [ ] 2 large carrots
- [ ] 1 large parsley root
- [ ] 1 small celeriac
- [ ] 3–4 medium potatoes (about 400 g total), peeled
- [ ] 1 heaped tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
- [ ] 1 tsp hot/smoked paprika
- [ ] 2 tsp table salt
- [ ] 1 tsp ground caraway
- [ ] 2 bay leaves
- [ ] 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
- [ ] 1 small tomato or 2tbsp tomato paste
- [ ] 2 kapia peppers
- [ ] 3 cloves garlic
- [ ] 2 tbsp pork lard (or oil)
- [ ] ½ bunch fresh parsley
- [ ] 2.5 - 3 liters water  
       For the csipetke (pinched noodles)
- [ ] 55g egg (about 1 small egg)
- [ ] 80g plain wheat flour
- [ ] 3g salt (a generous pinch)

Instructions

- [ ] Brown onions in lard in a large pot.
- [ ] Remove from heat, stir in both paprikas, add a splash of water.
- [ ] Add beef, garlic, salt, pepper, caraway, bay leaves; cook until meat turns
      opaque.
- [ ] Add tomato and pepper; cover with water and simmer until meat is almost
      tender.
- [ ] Add carrots, parsley root, celeriac, potatoes, parsley, celery leaves; add
      more water to reach soup level.
- [ ] Simmer until meat and vegetables are fully tender; adjust seasoning.
- [ ] Mix egg, flour, salt into a stiff dough; rest briefly.
- [ ] Pinch tiny pieces (csipetke) and let them dry slightly.
- [ ] Add csipetke to gently boiling soup; cook until they float and are cooked
      through.
