Gingerbread Cake

Ingredients  
For the Cake

- [ ] 390g all-purpose flour, spooned & leveled or weighed out
- [ ] 3/4 tsp salt
- [ ] 3 tsp baking powder
- [ ] 2 tsp cinnamon
- [ ] 1/2 tsp ground cloves
- [ ] 1 and 1/2 tsp ground ginger
- [ ] 1/4 tsp nutmeg
- [ ] 226g salted butter, softened at room temperature
- [ ] 160g brown sugar, packed
- [ ] 160g granulated sugar
- [ ] 4 large eggs, at room temperature
- [ ] 2 tsp vanilla
- [ ] 350g molasses
- [ ] 240g buttermilk, at room temperature  
       For the Frosting
- [ ] 226g brown butter, cooled to room temperature
- [ ] 250g mascarpone, cold
- [ ] 200g cream cheese, cold
- [ ] 210g powdered sugar
- [ ] 2 tsp vanilla
- [ ] Pinch of salt  
       For the Sugared Cranberries & Rosemary Trees
- [ ] 120g water
- [ ] 105g granulated sugar (for syrup)
- [ ] 210g granulated sugar (for rolling)
- [ ] 7 sprigs fresh rosemary
- [ ] 1/2 cup fresh or frozen cranberries  
       For the Filling
- [ ] 200g sour cherry jam

Instructions  
Prep  
Grease and flour one 30cm round pan. Preheat oven to 165°C.  
Make the Cake

- [ ] Whisk together the dry ingredients in a medium bowl (flour, salt, baking
      powder, cinnamon, cloves, ginger, nutmeg). Set aside.
- [ ] In a large mixing bowl, cream softened butter with a hand mixer. Add
      sugars and mix on high for about 2 minutes, until well creamed together.
- [ ] Add the eggs 2 at a time, mixing on medium until just combined.
- [ ] Add molasses and vanilla, mix until combined, scraping the sides of the
      bowl with a silicone spatula.
- [ ] Mix in 1/3 of the flour mixture on low speed. Add 1/3 of the buttermilk,
      then mix on low speed. Repeat until everything is just incorporated.  
       Bake
- [ ] Evenly distribute the batter between your prepared pans. Bake for 40-60
      minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean or with moist crumbs.
- [ ] Let cakes cool in the pans 10 minutes, then run a butter knife around the
      edges and invert layers onto cooling racks. Let them cool completely
      before frosting.  
       Make the Sugared Cranberries & Rosemary Trees
- [ ] Mix water and sugar in a medium pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to
      a simmer, whisking until sugar is dissolved.
- [ ] Remove from heat and let cool for a bit before dipping the rosemary in the
      mixture (this prevents browning). Set aside on parchment paper to dry for
      15 minutes.
- [ ] Add the cranberries to the syrup in the pot. Cover with a dish towel and
      let sit 10 minutes.
- [ ] Remove cranberries (save the syrup!) and set on parchment paper to dry for
      15-60 minutes.
- [ ] Roll both rosemary and cranberries in granulated sugar and set on fresh
      parchment to set for 10 minutes.  
       Make the Frosting
- [ ] Cream the cooled brown butter until fluffy, about 1 minute.
- [ ] Beat in powdered sugar, vanilla, and salt until smooth.
- [ ] Add cold cream cheese in chunks, mixing just until combined. Don't
      overbeat or it will thin out.  
       Assemble & Frost
- [ ] Place one cake layer on your cake stand and spread with a layer of
      frosting, pushing it slightly over the edges.
- [ ] Pipe a border of frosting around the edge, then spread sour cherry jam
      evenly within the border.
- [ ] Add the second cake layer upside down to create a level top.
- [ ] Use remaining frosting to cover the top and dot the sides for a naked cake
      effect. Smooth with a cake scraper.
- [ ] Decorate with sugared rosemary trees (place sprigs upside down) and
      cranberries. Sprinkle with white sanding sugar for a snowfall effect.  
       Serve & Store  
       - [ ] Chill assembled cake for at least 30 minutes before slicing. Store
      covered in the fridge for 4-6 days.
