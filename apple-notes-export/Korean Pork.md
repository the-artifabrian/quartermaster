Korean Pork

Ingredients

For the Pork and Vegetables:

- [ ] 500g pork shoulder, thinly sliced
- [ ] 1 medium zucchini, julienned
- [ ] 2 large carrots, julienned
- [ ] 3 green onions, chopped into 1-inch pieces
- [ ] 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

For the Sauce:

- [ ] 3 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
- [ ] 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- [ ] 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- [ ] 2 tablespoons brown sugar or honey
- [ ] 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- [ ] 1 teaspoon grated ginger
- [ ] 1 tablespoon white wine/mirin/rice wine

Instructions

- [ ] Prepare the Sauce  
       In a small bowl, mix together the gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, brown
      sugar or honey, minced garlic, grated ginger, mirin (if using), and
      gochugaru (if using) until well combined. Set aside.

- [ ] Cook the Pork  
       Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over
      medium-high heat. Add the thinly sliced pork in batches, and stir-fry
      until it's fully cooked and slightly browned. Remove the pork from the
      skillet and set aside.

- [ ] Cook the Vegetables  
       In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of vegetable oil if needed.
      Add the julienned zucchini and carrots, and stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes
      until they are just tender but still crisp.

- [ ] Combine Pork and Vegetables  
       Return the cooked pork to the skillet with the vegetables. Lower the heat
      to medium.

- [ ] Add the Sauce  
       Pour the prepared sauce over the pork and vegetables. Stir well to ensure
      everything is evenly coated with the sauce.

- [ ] Final Touches  
       Add the chopped green onions to the skillet. Stir-fry everything together
      for another 1-2 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

- [ ] Serve  
       Serve hot with a side of steamed rice or over rice for a complete meal.
