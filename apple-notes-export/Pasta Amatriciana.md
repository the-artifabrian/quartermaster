Pasta "Amatriciana" 

Ingredients

- [ ] 350g spaghetti or bucatini
- [ ] 200g pancetta, diced
- [ ] 1/2 red onion, grated
- [ ] 3 cloves garlic, chopped
- [ ] 5g salt
- [ ] 40g tomato paste
- [ ] 700g passata rustica (or 2 cans whole peeled tomatoes)
- [ ] 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
- [ ] Fresh basil leaves, torn or chiffonade
- [ ] Parmiggiano for serving

Instructions

- [ ] Bring a large pot of salted water to boil for the pasta.
- [ ] In a large skillet, cook the diced pancetta over medium heat until it's
      crispy and the fat has rendered, about 5-7 minutes.
- [ ] Add the chopped onion to the pancetta and cook until softened, about 5
      minutes.
- [ ] Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes, cooking for another minute
      until fragrant.
- [ ] Add tomato paste, cook for 1 minute.
- [ ] Pour in the passata, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for about
      15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
- [ ] While the sauce is simmering, cook the pasta in the boiling water until al
      dente, according to package instructions.
- [ ] Before draining the pasta, reserve 1 cup of the pasta cooking water.
- [ ] Drain the pasta and add it to the skillet with the sauce. Toss to combine,
      adding some of the reserved pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce.
- [ ] Remove from heat and stir in the grated Parmigiano-Reggiano and most of
      the fresh basil leaves.
- [ ] Serve the pasta in bowls, garnished with additional Parmigiano and the
      remaining basil leaves.
