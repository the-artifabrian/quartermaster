Coq au Vin

Ingredients

- [ ] 12 chicken drumsticks
- [ ] 700ml red wine
- [ ] 250ml chicken stock
- [ ] 200g thick-cut bacon or lardons, diced (or pancetta)
- [ ] 1 teaspoon salt
- [ ] 1 medium onion, diced
- [ ] 4 medium carrots (cut into 1-inch piece)
- [ ] 4 cloves garlic (minced)
- [ ] 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- [ ] 2-3 fresh thyme sprigs
- [ ] 250g mushrooms (thickly sliced)
- [ ] 250g pearl onions (peeled)
- [ ] 2 tablespoons flour
- [ ] 2 tablespoons softened butter
- [ ] 2 bay leaves
- [ ] freshly cracked black pepper

  Instructions

- [x] Pat chicken drumsticks dry, then season with salt and pepper.
- [x] Fry bacon/lardons until crispy, remove and set aside. Pour off excess fat,
      leaving about 2–3 tbsp in the pot.
- [x] Brown chicken in the bacon fat in batches until nicely browned, remove.
- [x] Add pearl onions, fry until lightly browned, remove and set aside.
- [x] Add diced onion, cook until translucent.
- [x] Add tomato paste, cook 1–2 minutes.
- [x] Add garlic, cook until fragrant (30–60 seconds).
- [x] Add butter and flour, cook 2 minutes, stirring.
- [x] Slowly add chicken stock, stirring to avoid lumps, then add red wine.
- [x] Add bay leaves, thyme, bacon, carrots, and chicken back to the pot.
- [x] Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook 20–30 minutes, until
      chicken is tender and sauce has slightly thickened.
- [x] In a frying pan, sear mushrooms in 1 tbsp oil over high heat
      until browned; add to the pot for the last 5–10 minutes of cooking.
- [x] Add the browned pearl onions back for the last 10 minutes (if you
      want them softer, add earlier).
- [x] Taste and adjust with salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar if needed.
- [x] Garnish with chopped parsley and serve.
